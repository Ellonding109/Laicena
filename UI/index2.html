Research

Our research teams investigate the safety, inner workings, and societal impacts of AI models – so that artificial intelligence has a positive impact as it becomes increasingly capable.

Research teams: Interpretability Alignment Societal Impacts Video AI Systems

Interpretability

The mission of the Interpretability team is to discover and understand how large language models work internally, as a foundation for AI safety and positive outcomes.

Alignment

The Alignment team works to understand the risks of AI models and develop ways to ensure that future ones remain helpful, honest, and harmless.

Societal Impacts

Working closely with the Laicena Policy and Safeguards teams, Societal Impacts is a technical research team that explores how AI is used in the real world.

Video AI Systems

Our core research focuses on developing advanced deep learning systems for video understanding, generation, and intelligent editing automation.

Can our models access and report on its own internal states? This research finds evidence for a limited but functional ability to introspect—a step toward understanding what's actually happening inside these models.

Tracing the thoughts of a large language model

Circuit tracing lets us watch the model think, uncovering a shared conceptual space where reasoning happens before being translated into output—suggesting the model can learn something in one context and apply it in another.
